Working the Steep Ground Below Roan Mountain

Bakersville is a small county seat with big terrain around it. The town sits along Cane Creek in a narrow bottom, and the land climbs hard from there toward Roan Mountain, one of the highest and wettest ridgelines in the Southern Appalachians. The rhododendron balds that draw crowds to the NC Rhododendron Festival every June sit on the same slopes that shed a lot of water onto the lots below.

That combination, steep grade and heavy rainfall, is why site work here lives or dies on drainage and slope control. From Burnsville we are on site in Bakersville in about half an hour, and we build with the runoff in mind from the first cut.
